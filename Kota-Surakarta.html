About Surakarta

Surakarta City, also known as Solo, is a city in Central Java, Indonesia, with an area of ​​44.04 km². In 2020, Surakarta had a population of 522,364, with a density of 11,861 people/km². By the end of 2024, Surakarta's population was projected to reach 589,242.

This city is the third-largest city in southern Java, after Malang in East Java and Bandung in West Java, by population. The eastern side of the city is crossed by the river immortalized in one of the keroncong songs, Bengawan Solo. The city is part of the Greater Solo region, where it serves as the main city.

Along with Yogyakarta, Surakarta was the heir to the Islamic Mataram Kingdom, which was divided through the Treaty of Giyanti in 1755, making Surakarta the residence of Susuhunan Pakubuwana and Adipati Mangkunegara.

Top Tourist Attractions

Kraton Kasunanan (Keraton Surakarta)

The royal palace complex where you can see Javanese architecture, royal artifacts, and sometimes traditional performances.

Pura Mangkunegaran

A smaller princely palace with beautiful pendopo halls and a museum of Mangkunegaran treasures.

Batik Museums & Batik Villages

Surakarta is famous for batik. Visit museums and batik neighborhoods like Laweyan and Kauman to see artisans at work and buy authentic textiles.

Pasar Gede

A historic traditional market where you can taste local food and experience daily life in Solo.

Taman Sriwedari

Sriwedari Park is a park located in Laweyan District, Surakarta City. Since the era of Pakubuwana X, Sriwedari Park has been the venue for the traditional entertainment of "Night of Selikuran."

Museum Sangiran

Sangiran Museum of Early Man is an archaeological museum located in two districts, namely Sragen and Karanganyar.
